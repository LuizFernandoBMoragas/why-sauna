The Mechanism

What happens to your body at 90°C

The benefits are not mysterious. They are the result of five specific physiological responses — each well-documented, each measurable, each cumulative with regular use.

❤️
Cardiovascular conditioning
At 90–100°C, your heart rate rises to 100–150 bpm — equivalent to moderate aerobic exercise. Cardiac output increases by 60–70%. Done regularly, this trains your cardiovascular system without joint stress. The European Journal of Preventive Cardiology (2022) confirmed this in a meta-analysis of 12 controlled studies.
🧬
Heat shock proteins & cellular repair
Intense heat triggers the production of heat shock proteins (HSPs), which repair damaged cellular proteins and reduce systemic inflammation. This mechanism is directly linked to the reduced risk of neurodegenerative disease observed in long-term sauna users.
💪
Growth hormone surge
A single sauna session increases growth hormone levels by 200–300% (University of Turku, 2018). This supports muscle preservation, fat metabolism, and recovery — particularly relevant after the age of 40, when natural GH production declines significantly.
🌙
Parasympathetic activation & sleep
The cooling phase after sauna activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing cortisol and adrenaline. This is the physiological reason why 83% of regular sauna users report improved sleep quality. The effect is not psychological — it is a measurable hormonal shift.
☀️
Thermoregulatory adaptation — the Malta factor
Regular sauna use in warm climates improves your body's efficiency at cooling itself. Users in hot countries consistently report feeling more comfortable in summer after 4–6 weeks of regular sauna use — not less. The sauna trains your thermoregulatory system the same way exercise trains your cardiovascular system.
💧
Löyly — the Finnish mechanism
Throwing water on hot stones (löyly) creates a brief burst of steam that raises perceived temperature without raising dry air temperature. This intensifies the cardiovascular response while keeping the sauna comfortable to breathe in. It is the defining feature of a real Finnish sauna — and the reason infrared and steam rooms cannot replicate the results.
